title = "Code of Conduct"
+++

We are committed to ensuring that all discussion, meeting, or events organized during this ThatCamp, are free from harassment including but not limited to restrictions on free expression, discrimination against any person on the basis of race, color, sex, religion, national origin, age, sexual orientation, disability, gender identity or expression, marital status, genetic predisposition or carrier status, military status, and beyond. We do not tolerate harassment or disrespectful treatment of group participants in any form. We welcome and encourage intellectual discussion, debate, civil and active engagement.  


We value individual positionality in articulating statements and beliefs more than being “all on the same page.” Rather than striving to agree, we require articulation, explanation, asking questions, working respectfully across differences, showing compassion and understanding.  

## Reporting Guidelines  

Please report via email to: Anna Kijas (anna.kijas@tufts.edu), Kaylen Dwyer (kaylen.dwyer@tufts.edu), and Ece Turnator (turnator@mit.edu)  

In your report please include:  
- Your contact info (so we can get in touch with you if we need to follow up)
- Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved. If there were other witnesses besides you, please try to include them as well.
- During which session did this event happen? Please be as specific as possible.
- Your account of what occurred.
- Any extra context you believe existed for the incident.
- Any other information you believe we should have.



_The core of the Statement of Values and Code of Conduct section of this document is adapted from the [DHSI Statement on Ethics and Inclusion](http://www.dhsi.org/events.php), [Carpentries Code of Conduct](https://docs.carpentries.org/policies/coc/), and the [HILT Statement of Values.](https://dhtraining.org/hilt/about/values/)_
